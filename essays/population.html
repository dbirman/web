(Over?) Population


 I'm always surprised by how rarely I hear about overpopulation as a problem on our planet. I want to write down here my thoughts on why I strongly believe we should have limits on the number of children people can have, and my thoughts on a number of semi-related issues.

 If you read my morality essay you'll know that I believe that we have a moral imperative to maintain the existence and expansion of consciousness. An important caveat to this is that we shouldn't be doing so at the cost of creating suffering--which as I define elsewhere is an experience that indicates to a conscious organism that its existence is at risk. In short: we should be making more conscious organisms, similar to us or different, but not so many that we deplete our own resources or put ourselves at risk. So when I look at the world and see eight billion humans and many more billions of animals, with around 50+% suffering at any given moment, I feel our current situation is misaligned with our ultimate goals. A priority for us should be to stabilize population at a level where all organisms can minimize level 1 suffering (see Morality).
