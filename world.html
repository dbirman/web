Research Projects

MT is not alone. (aka The neural representations of contrast and motion coherence and the influence of feature-based attention.

Here we test how feature-based attention to contrast and motion coherence alter the perceptions of these features and the neural representations. We model both behavior and neural responses together to investigate how feature-based attention alters neural properties for these important visual attributes. Our findings suggest that MT is not alone in representing motion, and that while attention to contrast alters the perception of motion the opposite is not true. Attention Awareness

What is it like to be a monkey? (aka Abstract Representations in Perceptual Tasks)

Monkeys often take much longer to train on simple perceptual tasks than humans. For example, in learning to 'match' two samples, or to hold in mind the color of an incoherent stimulus, monkeys can take months to achieve stable performance. Even after extensive training monkey performance sometimes appears qualitatively different from human performance. Is this because monkeys are doing these tasks in a different way? Here we leverage a modeling approach to compare monkey and human behavior and use these models to gain insight into neural dynamics and the computations underlying perceptual decision making.
