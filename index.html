Project Highlight:

Neural Substrates of Attention and Awareness

with Justin Gardner

Everybody has experienced "flow": moments where we are intensely focused on a single aspect of the world, while the rest of the world fades way. I am studying how attention alters our perceptions in these situations. In particular, it should be possible to map out the circuits that invoke and diminish perception, respectively, during moments of intense concentration.

Project Highlight:

Controlling Predictive Brain Signals

with Matthias-Schultze Kraft, John-Dylan Haynes, and others

It's now well known that brain activation precedes our sense of conscious awareness. But is that brain activity part of an unmalleable causal chain? Our research suggests that there is an unavoidable chain of events far in advance of each movement. even within the last hundred milliseconds other inhibitory processes can still stop the movement.
